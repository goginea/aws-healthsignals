# CDC COVID-19 Guidance (Current: 2025–26 Season)

*Source: CDC Updated COVID-19 Guidance (post-Public Health Emergency), CDC Respiratory Virus Guidance (March 2024 update), CDC Paxlovid Prescribing Guidance, MMWR COVID-19 Surveillance Reports*

---

## Current Isolation and Respiratory Virus Guidance

*Updated per CDC's unified respiratory virus guidance (effective March 2024):*

### When to Stay Home:
A person with COVID-19 (or any respiratory virus) should stay home and away from others when they have:
- Fever (100.4°F / 38°C or higher) **OR**
- Symptoms are not improving

### When to Resume Normal Activities:
Resume normal activities when **BOTH** of the following are true for at least 24 hours (without fever-reducing medication):
1. Fever-free for ≥24 hours
2. Symptoms are improving overall

### Additional Precautions After Returning:
For the next 5 days after resuming activities:
- Wear a well-fitting mask indoors around others
- Maintain physical distance when possible
- Improve ventilation in shared spaces
- Practice enhanced hand hygiene

### Key Change from Prior Guidance:
The 5-day mandatory isolation is NO LONGER recommended. The new approach is symptom-based and applies to COVID-19, influenza, and RSV equally.

## Antiviral Treatment

### Paxlovid (Nirmatrelvir/Ritonavir):
- **Indication**: Adults and adolescents ≥12 years (≥40 kg) with mild-to-moderate COVID-19 at HIGH RISK for progression
- **Timing**: Must be initiated within **5 days of symptom onset** — earlier is more effective
- **Duration**: 5 days (complete full course)
- **Efficacy**: ~89% reduction in hospitalization/death when started within 5 days
- **Drug interactions**: SIGNIFICANT — check all current medications (CYP3A4 inhibitor)
- **Rebound**: Possible symptom rebound after completion (~10–15%) — does NOT require retreatment in most cases

### High-Risk Factors for Progression:
- Age ≥65 years
- Immunocompromised status
- Chronic lung disease (COPD, asthma)
- Cardiovascular disease
- Diabetes
- Obesity (BMI ≥30)
- Chronic kidney disease
- Current cancer/active treatment
- Sickle cell disease
- Neurological conditions affecting breathing
- Pregnancy

### Rural Considerations for Antiviral Access:
- Ensure local pharmacies maintain Paxlovid stock before season
- Establish telehealth-to-prescription pathway (patient calls → provider prescribes → pharmacy dispenses)
- Test-to-treat sites reduce delay between diagnosis and treatment
- 5-day window is CRITICAL — rural patients often delay seeking care by 2-3 days

## Wastewater Signal Interpretation

### What Wastewater Data Shows:
- SARS-CoV-2 RNA levels in community wastewater
- Detects infection 4–6 days BEFORE clinical cases increase
- Captures asymptomatic and unreported infections
- Does NOT indicate severity — only viral circulation

### Wastewater Viral Activity Level (WVAL) Categories:
| Level | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Very Low | Minimal viral circulation | Routine monitoring |
| Low | Some virus present, below historical average | Enhanced awareness |
| Moderate | Virus at typical seasonal levels | Recommend masking for high-risk |
| High | Virus above historical average | Active community messaging, ensure antivirals stocked |
| Very High | Virus well above historical — surge likely | Healthcare system alert, activate surge protocols |

### Limitations:
- Cannot distinguish between variants
- Does not indicate disease severity
- Not all communities have wastewater monitoring sites
- Results lag actual infection by 2-4 days (collection + lab processing)

## Variant Monitoring Considerations

### Current Approach (2025-26):
- CDC monitors variant proportions through genomic surveillance (Traveler-Based Genomic Surveillance + NSSP)
- Variants are classified as: Variants Being Monitored (VBM), Variants of Interest (VOI), Variants of Concern (VOC), Variants of High Consequence (VOHC)
- Updated XBB.1.5-derived vaccine formulation targets current dominant variants

### What Local Health Departments Need to Know:
- Variant information does NOT change local preparedness actions
- All current variants respond to Paxlovid treatment
- Vaccines provide cross-protection (reduced but still effective against variant drift)
- Focus on CIRCULATION LEVEL, not variant identity, for local decision-making
- If CDC declares a new VOC: await updated guidance before changing local protocols

## Vulnerable Population Protections

### Priority Populations for COVID-19 Preparedness:
1. Long-term care facility residents (vaccination + infection control)
2. Immunocompromised persons (may need additional vaccine doses)
3. Unvaccinated elderly (≥75 years)
4. Persons experiencing homelessness
5. Congregate living settings (shelters, correctional facilities, group homes)
6. Communities with limited healthcare access (rural, tribal)

### Actions for Local Health Departments:
- Verify vaccination rates in long-term care facilities (target: >90% residents, >75% staff)
- Ensure antiviral access pathways for high-risk populations
- Pre-position COVID-19 rapid tests at senior centers, food banks, shelters
- Maintain relationships with community health workers serving vulnerable populations
- Plan for respiratory illness isolation capacity in homeless shelters

## Testing Strategy Recommendations

### Current CDC Testing Guidance (Post-PHE):
- Testing recommended for: symptomatic persons at high risk, symptomatic healthcare workers, before antivirals
- Routine screening of asymptomatic persons: NO LONGER recommended in most settings
- Exception: Healthcare facilities and congregate settings may continue screening protocols

### Test Types Available:
| Test Type | Turnaround | Sensitivity | Best Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rapid Antigen (OTC) | 15 minutes | 60–80% | Symptomatic persons day 2-5 |
| PCR (lab-based) | 24-72 hours | >95% | Confirmation, outbreak investigation |
| Multiplex PCR | 24-72 hours | >95% | Differentiating flu/RSV/COVID |
| Wastewater PCR | Weekly batch | Population-level | Community surveillance only |

### Rural Testing Considerations:
- Stock rapid antigen tests at health department for distribution
- Establish relationship with nearest PCR lab (turnaround time matters for Paxlovid window)
- Consider multiplex panels (test for flu + COVID + RSV simultaneously) — single swab
- Home test distribution through community sites (libraries, post offices, food pantries)
