# Public Announcement Templates

*Templates for communications that county health officers send to THEIR communities. Written at 8th grade reading level. Adapted from real public health department communications.*

---

## 1. Press Release Template (Local Newspaper)

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
[DATE]

Contact: [HEALTH_OFFICER_NAME], [TITLE]
[COUNTY_NAME] Health Department
Phone: [PHONE] | Email: [EMAIL]

[COUNTY_NAME] HEALTH DEPARTMENT ISSUES [DISEASE] PREPARATION ADVISORY

[CITY], [STATE] — The [COUNTY_NAME] Health Department is advising 
residents to prepare for increased [DISEASE] activity expected in 
the coming [LAG_WEEKS] weeks. This advisory is based on disease 
monitoring data showing elevated [DISEASE] activity in the 
[METRO_NAME] metropolitan area.

"[DISEASE] activity in [METRO_NAME] has reached levels that 
historically precede increased illness in our community," said 
[HEALTH_OFFICER_NAME], [TITLE] of [COUNTY_NAME] Health Department. 
"This gives us valuable preparation time. We are sharing this 
information so residents and healthcare providers can take steps now 
to protect our community."

WHAT RESIDENTS SHOULD DO:
• Get vaccinated if you haven't already — it's not too late
• Stock up on over-the-counter fever and cold medications
• If you are high-risk (over 65, chronic illness, pregnant), talk 
  to your doctor about antiviral medications
• Wash hands frequently and stay home when you feel sick
• [ADDITIONAL_ACTIONS]

WHAT THE HEALTH DEPARTMENT IS DOING:
• Monitoring disease activity daily through surveillance systems
• Coordinating with local healthcare providers and hospitals
• Ensuring antiviral medications are available at local pharmacies
• Preparing surge staffing plans if needed
• [ADDITIONAL_DEPARTMENT_ACTIONS]

This advisory does NOT mean [DISEASE] is currently spreading in 
[COUNTY_NAME]. It is an early warning based on historical patterns 
that gives our community time to prepare. [COUNTY_NAME] Health 
Department will provide updates as the situation develops.

For more information:
• [COUNTY_NAME] Health Department: [PHONE]
• CDC information on [DISEASE]: [CDC_URL]
• Vaccine locations: [VACCINE_URL]

###
```

## 2. Social Media Post Templates (Facebook)

```

early activity — this is normal for this time of year.

NOW is a great time to:
✅ Get your [DISEASE] vaccine
✅ Stock up on tissues, hand sanitizer, and fever reducers
✅ Review your family's sick-day plan

No action needed yet for [COUNTY_NAME] — we're just giving you a 
heads up! We'll keep you posted.

Questions? Call us: [PHONE]
#[COUNTY_NAME]Health #[DISEASE]Season #BePrepared
```

### Advisory (MODERATE):
```
⚠️ [DISEASE] PREPARATION ADVISORY

[COUNTY_NAME] Health Department is advising residents to prepare for 
[DISEASE] activity expected in approximately [LAG_WEEKS] weeks.

[METRO_NAME] is currently experiencing elevated [DISEASE] activity. 
Based on past years, this typically reaches our area [LAG_WEEKS] 
weeks later.

WHAT YOU CAN DO NOW:
1️⃣ Get vaccinated — it takes 2 weeks to build protection
2️⃣ Talk to your doctor if you're high-risk
3️⃣ Stock basic supplies (fever medicine, tissues, fluids)
4️⃣ Make a plan for staying home if you get sick

This is NOT an emergency — this is preparation time. That's the 
whole point of early warning.

More info: [LINK]
Questions: [PHONE]

#[COUNTY_NAME]Health #PrepareNotPanic #[DISEASE]Advisory
```

### Urgent (HIGH):
```
🔴 URGENT HEALTH ADVISORY — [DISEASE]

[COUNTY_NAME] Health Department has issued a HIGH severity advisory 
for [DISEASE]. We expect significant activity in our area within 
[LAG_WEEKS] weeks.

TAKE ACTION NOW:
🔴 Get vaccinated TODAY if not already (call [VACCINE_PHONE])
🔴 High-risk individuals: contact your provider about antivirals
🔴 Stock 2 weeks of medications and supplies
🔴 If you feel sick: STAY HOME. Call before visiting a clinic.
🔴 Watch for updates from our department

WHO IS HIGH-RISK: Adults over 65, pregnant women, young children, 
anyone with chronic conditions (diabetes, heart disease, COPD)

We are working closely with local clinics and the state health 
department. This advisory is based on historical data — it gives 
us time to prepare. Let's use that time wisely.

Call us with questions: [PHONE]
Updates: [WEBSITE]

#[COUNTY_NAME]Health #[DISEASE]Alert #ProtectYourFamily
```

## 3. Community Meeting Talking Points

```
COMMUNITY MEETING TALKING POINTS
[DISEASE] Preparedness — [COUNTY_NAME]
Date: [DATE]

OPENING (2 minutes):
• Thank everyone for attending
• Introduce yourself and role
• "I have important information to share AND good news — we have 
  time to prepare"

KEY MESSAGE 1 — THE SITUATION (3 minutes):
• "[DISEASE] activity is [elevated/high] in [METRO_NAME]"
• "In past years, when [METRO_NAME] sees this, our area follows 
  about [LAG_WEEKS] weeks later"
• "This year's signal is [SEVERITY_DESCRIPTION]"
• "This is NOT a reason to panic — it IS a reason to prepare"

KEY MESSAGE 2 — WHAT WE'RE DOING (3 minutes):
• "Your health department is [ACTIONS_BEING_TAKEN]"
• "We're working with [PARTNERS]"
• "We have supplies ready and plans in place"
• "We will communicate regularly as the situation develops"

KEY MESSAGE 3 — WHAT YOU CAN DO (5 minutes):
• "Get vaccinated — it takes 2 weeks to work"
• "Stock basic supplies at home"
• "Know the signs that need medical attention: [WARNING_SIGNS]"
• "Have a plan to stay home for a few days if you get sick"
• "Check on elderly neighbors and family members"

CLOSING (2 minutes):
• "We're telling you early so you can prepare — not so you worry"
• "We will send updates by [CHANNELS]"
• "Questions? Call us at [PHONE]"
• "Thank you for helping keep [COUNTY_NAME] healthy"

ANTICIPATED QUESTIONS:
Q: "Should I be scared?"
A: "No. This is preparation time — that's valuable. Most people 
   recover at home with rest and fluids."

Q: "Should I keep kids home from school?"
A: "Not yet. We'll communicate clearly if that changes."

Q: "Is the vaccine safe/effective?"
A: "Yes. [Brief facts]. Talk to your doctor with specific concerns."

Q: "How accurate is this prediction?"
A: "Based on [SEASONS_COUNT] years of data. Our confidence is 
   [CONFIDENCE]%. We may be early or late by a week or two."
```

## 4. School Notification Letter Template

```
[SCHOOL_DISTRICT_LETTERHEAD]

[DATE]

Dear Parents and Guardians,

The [COUNTY_NAME] Health Department has notified us of an upcoming 
[DISEASE] advisory for our area. Based on disease monitoring, 
[DISEASE] activity may increase in our community in the coming 
[LAG_WEEKS] weeks.

WHAT THIS MEANS FOR SCHOOL:
• School remains OPEN and operating normally
• We are increasing cleaning of high-touch surfaces
• Hand sanitizer stations have been restocked
• School nurses are monitoring for increased illness

WHAT WE ASK OF FAMILIES:
• Keep children home if they have a fever (100.4°F or higher) 
  OR persistent cough
• Children may return when fever-free for 24 hours WITHOUT 
  fever-reducing medication
• Consider getting your child vaccinated if not already done
• Send children with tissues and water bottles
• Update emergency contacts if any have changed

IF YOUR CHILD IS HIGH-RISK:
If your child has asthma, diabetes, or immune system conditions, 
please:
• Ensure we have current medications and care plans on file
• Talk with your child's doctor about additional precautions
• Let the school nurse know of any concerns: [NURSE_PHONE]

WE WILL NOTIFY YOU IF:
• Significant absenteeism occurs (>10% school-wide)
• School closure is being considered
• Any change to school operations

Questions? Contact:
• School nurse: [NURSE_PHONE]
• [COUNTY_NAME] Health Department: [HEALTH_DEPT_PHONE]

Thank you for partnering with us to keep our school community 
healthy.

Sincerely,
[SUPERINTENDENT_NAME]
Superintendent, [SCHOOL_DISTRICT]
```

## 5. Healthcare Provider Advisory Template

```
HEALTHCARE PROVIDER ADVISORY
[COUNTY_NAME] Health Department
Date: [DATE] | Priority: [SEVERITY]

RE: [DISEASE] Preparation Advisory — Expected Activity in [LAG_WEEKS] Weeks

Dear Healthcare Colleagues,

Based on surveillance data from metropolitan sentinel sites, the 
[COUNTY_NAME] Health Department anticipates increased [DISEASE] 
activity in our service area within approximately [LAG_WEEKS] weeks.

CLINICAL PREPARATION RECOMMENDATIONS:

1. TESTING:
   • Ensure rapid [DISEASE] test supply (recommend [QUANTITY]+ kits)
   • Consider multiplex panels (flu/COVID/RSV) for efficiency
   • Remind staff of specimen collection procedures

2. TREATMENT:
   • Verify antiviral stock: [ANTIVIRAL_DETAILS]
   • Review prescribing criteria and drug interactions
   • Establish telehealth pathway for low-acuity patients
   • [TREATMENT_WINDOW_REMINDER]

3. INFECTION CONTROL:
   • Review respiratory isolation procedures with staff
   • Ensure PPE supply for [DURATION]-week surge
   • Designate respiratory illness waiting area if not already done
   • Update sick-leave policies to encourage staff staying home

4. PATIENT COMMUNICATION:
   • Proactively contact high-risk patients about vaccination
   • Set up nurse triage line for after-hours calls
   • Prepare patient handouts on when to seek care vs. self-care

5. STAFFING:
   • Assess staff vaccination status
   • Review contingency staffing plans
   • Consider temporary staff requests now (agencies have 2-3 week lead time)

REPORTING REQUIREMENTS:
• Report clusters of severe respiratory illness to county health dept
• Pediatric influenza deaths: mandatory 24-hour reporting to state
• Novel influenza A: immediate reporting to state epidemiologist

RESOURCES:
• Current CDC clinical guidance: [CDC_URL]
• County health department: [PHONE] | [EMAIL]
• State epidemiologist: [STATE_EPI_PHONE]
• Hospital bed availability: [HOSPITAL_SYSTEM_PHONE]

This advisory will be updated [WEEKLY/AS_NEEDED]. Reply to confirm 
receipt or with questions.

[HEALTH_OFFICER_NAME], [CREDENTIALS]
[TITLE], [COUNTY_NAME] Health Department
```
