# CDC Influenza Preparedness Guidance for Local Health Departments

*Source: CDC Community Mitigation Guidelines to Prevent Pandemic Influenza (2017), CDC Pandemic Influenza Plan Update (2017), CDC Influenza Planning Guide for Local Health Departments*

---

## Influenza-Like Illness (ILI) Definition

CDC defines ILI as:
- Fever ≥100°F (37.8°C) **AND**
- Cough and/or sore throat
- In the absence of a known cause other than influenza

For syndromic surveillance (NSSP), the definition expands to include:
- Emergency department chief complaints mentioning fever/chills AND (cough OR sore throat OR upper respiratory infection)
- OR discharge diagnosis of influenza

## Activity Level Triggers for Local Action

| CDC Activity Level | % ED Visits (ILI) | Recommended Local Action |
|---|---|---|
| Minimal | <1.0% | Routine surveillance; no additional action |
| Low | 1.0–2.4% | Enhance surveillance; verify vaccine supply |
| Moderate | 2.5–4.9% | Activate preparedness plan; begin community communication |
| High | 5.0–7.9% | Implement mitigation measures; surge staffing |
| Very High | ≥8.0% | Full emergency operations; consider school closures |

## Recommended Stockpile Items for Rural Facilities

### Minimum 4-Week Supply (per CDC Pandemic Planning Checklist):

**Medical Supplies:**
- N95 respirators: 50 per staff member
- Surgical masks: 200 per staff member
- Examination gloves (nitrile): 500 pairs per staff member
- Hand sanitizer (62%+ alcohol): 10 gallons per facility
- Antiviral medications (oseltamivir): 2-week treatment courses × projected patient volume
- Rapid influenza diagnostic tests (RIDTs): 200 per facility minimum
- Pulse oximeters: 5 per clinical area
- IV fluids (normal saline, lactated Ringer's): 100 liters
- Acetaminophen/ibuprofen: 500 doses each
- Specimen collection swabs (nasopharyngeal): 500

**Operational Supplies:**
- Cleaning/disinfection supplies (EPA-registered): 30-day supply
- Biohazard bags: 500
- Thermometers (non-contact preferred): 10 per facility
- Signage materials for respiratory hygiene stations
- Patient education materials (multi-language)

## Staffing Surge Guidance for Facilities with <10 Staff

### Immediate Actions (within 48 hours of reaching "Moderate" level):
1. Activate cross-training protocols — every staff member should be able to perform basic triage
2. Contact Medical Reserve Corps (MRC) for volunteer activation
3. Establish 12-hour shift rotations (from standard 8-hour)
4. Cancel all non-essential meetings, training, and administrative activities
5. Establish telehealth triage line to reduce in-person visits

### Staffing Tiers:
- **Tier 1** (Moderate activity): Extend hours, cancel leave, activate 1-2 MRC volunteers
- **Tier 2** (High activity): 12-hour shifts, activate all available MRC, request mutual aid
- **Tier 3** (Very High): Emergency staffing — all hands, request state epidemiologist support, activate Emergency Management Assistance Compact (EMAC) if needed

### Mutual Aid Activation:
- Contact neighboring county health departments within 50-mile radius
- Notify Regional Healthcare Coalition (RHC)
- Request state health department deployment assistance
- Timeline: mutual aid requests should be made ≥2 weeks before anticipated peak

## Community Mitigation Measures by Severity

### Pandemic Severity Index (adapted from CDC 2017 Community Mitigation Guidelines):

**Category A (Low severity — seasonal-like):**
- Voluntary home isolation of ill persons (recommend stay home when symptomatic)
- Respiratory hygiene/cough etiquette messaging
- Hand hygiene promotion
- Routine cleaning of high-touch surfaces

**Category B (Moderate severity):**
- All Category A measures
- Voluntary home quarantine of household contacts of ill persons
- Recommend telework where feasible
- Reduce non-essential gatherings
- Social distancing in workplaces (6-foot spacing)

**Category C (High severity):**
- All Category A and B measures
- Recommend dismissal of childcare programs and schools for up to 4 weeks
- Modify, postpone, or cancel large public gatherings
- Recommend social distancing measures for workplaces
- Consider closing community centers, libraries, recreation facilities

**Category D (Very High / Catastrophic):**
- All previous measures
- Mandatory school closures
- Cancel all public gatherings
- Recommend shelter-in-place for high-risk populations
- Coordinate with law enforcement for enforcement of public health orders

## Vaccination Campaign Timing

### Seasonal Influenza:
- **Optimal vaccination period**: September–October (before community transmission begins)
- **Late vaccination still beneficial**: Through January or later
- **Children 6 months–8 years (first vaccination)**: Need 2 doses, 4 weeks apart — start by early September
- **High-risk priority groups**: Healthcare workers, adults ≥65, pregnant women, children 6-59 months, persons with chronic conditions

### During Active Outbreak (when HealthSignals alert fires):
- Accelerate vaccination of unvaccinated persons (it takes 2 weeks for protection)
- If ≥3 weeks before expected county peak: vaccination still worthwhile
- If <2 weeks before expected peak: focus on antiviral prophylaxis for high-risk
- Coordinate with pharmacies for extended-hours vaccination clinics

## Emergency Operations Activation Criteria

Activate local Emergency Operations Center (EOC) when ANY of the following occur:
1. ILI ED visit percentage exceeds 5.0% in sentinel metro (from HealthSignals alert)
2. More than 3 confirmed influenza-related hospitalizations in 7 days (in county)
3. State health department issues official health alert/advisory
4. Healthcare facility reports staffing below 75% due to illness
5. ICU occupancy exceeds 85% at nearest hospital
6. Antiviral supply drops below 2-week projected demand

### EOC Activation Levels:
- **Level 3 (Monitoring)**: Enhanced surveillance, situation awareness — ≥1 staff dedicated
- **Level 2 (Partial)**: Key positions staffed, coordination with state — 12-hour operations
- **Level 1 (Full)**: All positions staffed, 24-hour operations, state/federal coordination

## School Closure Decision Framework

*Source: CDC "Getting Ready for School Dismissals" and Community Mitigation Guidelines*

### Decision Criteria (consider closure when ≥2 of the following):
1. Absenteeism exceeds 10% (all-cause) OR 5% (ILI-specific)
2. CDC-confirmed high or very high severity
3. Disproportionate pediatric hospitalizations or deaths
4. Insufficient substitute teachers/staff to maintain operations
5. State or local health department recommendation

### Pre-Closure Actions:
- Notify parents 24-48 hours before planned closure (when possible)
- Arrange meal distribution for free/reduced lunch students
- Establish remote learning alternatives
- Coordinate childcare for healthcare worker parents
- Plan for students with special needs requiring in-person services

### Duration Guidance:
- Minimum recommended closure: 5-7 days (one incubation period)
- Typical pandemic closure: 2-4 weeks
- Reassess weekly based on transmission indicators
- Resume when community transmission shows sustained decline (2 consecutive weeks)

### Rural-Specific Considerations:
- School bus routes may serve as disease transmission corridors
- Many rural schools serve as community gathering places — closure affects multiple functions
- Agricultural calendar may affect timing decisions
- Communication must reach families without reliable internet (use school call systems, local radio)
